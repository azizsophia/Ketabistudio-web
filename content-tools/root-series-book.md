## Spread 01 — رحم · mercy and the womb
**Hook:** In Arabic, mercy and the womb come from the same root.
**Root:** ر ح م → *raḥim* (the womb), *raḥma* (mercy); *Ar-Raḥmān*, *Ar-Raḥīm* derive from it.
**Payoff:** Not a coincidence noticed by grammarians. Allah says He named the womb
after His own name. And you speak that mercy, named twice, before everything, in
the Bismillah that opens 113 of the Qur'an's 114 surahs.
**Source:** Jami' at-Tirmidhi 1907 (graded sound), narrated 'Abdur-Rahman bin
'Awf — "I created the Rahim (womb), and named it after My Name." sunnah.com/tirmidhi:1907 ·
root: corpus.quran.com (ر ح م) · basmala opens all surahs but At-Tawbah (9).
**Response so far:** Carousel 01 (live 07-04). Substack post 1 "Mercy, in its
mother tongue" (live 07-05); drew a deeply personal reader comment (Siti Rahmah)
we answered. Strong emotional resonance. **Flagship chapter.**

## Spread 02 — قلب · the heart that turns
**Hook:** The Arabic word for "heart" comes from a root meaning: to turn over.
**Root:** ق ل ب → *qalb* (heart), *qalaba* (to flip/overturn), *taqallub* (the
heart's turning between states).
**Payoff:** Your heart is named after its own nature: always turning, never still.
So when it won't hold still, that is not your failure, it is the meaning of the
word. The Prophet ﷺ often turned to Allah as *Muqallib al-qulub*, the Turner of
hearts: "Ya Muqallib al-qulub, thabbit qalbi ala dinik."
**Source:** du'a — Jami' at-Tirmidhi 2140 (graded ḥasan) · root: corpus.quran.com (ق ل ب).
**Response so far:** Carousel 02, queued as the 07-08 anchor. (Update after it posts.)
