# qalb Threads text-thread (paste as connected posts, ≤500 chars each)

**1/**
In Arabic, the word for your heart is qalb.

It comes from a root that doesn't mean anything soft or still.

ق ل ب means: to turn something over. to flip it.

your heart is literally named "the thing that turns." 🧵

**2/**
qalaba is what you do to a coin or a page: turn it to its other side.

taqallub is the turning of a thing back and forth between states.

so the heart isn't named for love or warmth. it's named for its motion. the one organ that will not sit still.

**3/**
so sit with this for a second:

if the word for your heart means "the thing that turns," then a heart that keeps turning was never broken. it was working.

the nearness that comes and goes. the faith that rises and falls. that isn't a flaw. it's the meaning of the word.

**4/**
and the Prophet ﷺ, with the most steadfast heart of anyone, didn't pretend otherwise.

he asked, again and again:

Ya Muqallib al-qulub, thabbit qalbi ala dinik.
O Turner of hearts, keep my heart firm upon Your religion.

he didn't ask for a heart that never moves. he asked the One who turns it to hold it steady.

**5/**
so when your heart swings away from Him, don't read it as a verdict on your faith. read it as the meaning of the word.

hand the turning back to the only One who can steady it.

save this for a restless day. 🤍

(source: root ق ل ب, corpus.quran.com; du'a Tirmidhi 2140, ḥasan)
